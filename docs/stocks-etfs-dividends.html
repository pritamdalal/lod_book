1.1 Stocks, ETFs, & Dividends

Issuing shares of stock is a way that companies raise funds for growing their business. The owner of a stock is entitled to a fraction of the companies profits and also has the ability to vote on certain matters at shareholder meetings. A crucial point is that the owner of a stock has limited liability in the rest of the company, meaning the worst thing that can happen is that shareholder never recieves any profits, and the stock itself also becomes worthless. In this sense, the value of stock can never be negative.

Once a stock has been issued by a company, the owner of the stock can sell it to any other entity. This is called a secondary market transaction. Once the share has been sold in the secondary market, the claims to profits, and the ability to both are both transfered to whoever purchased it.

Secondary market transactions occur on exchanges, like the New York Stock Exchange, and are usually what people are referring to when they talk about the stock market. These transactions occur via complicated auction mechanisms that match buyers and sellers. The mechanisms are such that the actual trade prices can fluctuate (sometimes greatly) from transaction to transaction.

Suppose you want to trade a share of stock, and you want to be able to fill your trade immediately. If you are a buyer of the stock, you will be charged a certain amount called the ask price. If you are a seller of the stock you will recieve a certain amount called the bid price. The bid price is lower than the ask price.

If you are a market for a particular stock, there are three prices that you will surely need to keep your eye one. The current bid price, the current ask price, and the last price at which trade occurred. We will often refer to the price of a stock at time \(T\) and denote by \(S_{T}\). While we usually gloss over what exactly this means, you can choose to interpret it as the average of the bid and ask at time \(T\), or the last trade price at time \(T\). In stocks that have high trading volume, these two values will be quite close - but this is not always the case.
